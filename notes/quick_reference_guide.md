# Quick Reference Guide: Therapeutic Time-Blocking Methodology

## At-a-Glance Session Structure

### Foundation Setting Block (Sessions 1-3)
- **Check-in**: Emotional temperature (5 min)
- **Relationship Building**: Safety & trust (10 min) 
- **Psychoeducation**: IPNB/ACT concepts (15 min)
- **Practice**: Mindfulness/regulation skills (10 min)
- **Processing**: Reflection & planning (10 min)

### Awareness & Understanding Block (Sessions 4-8)
- **Check-in**: IPNB lens (5 min)
- **Exploration**: Relationship patterns (15 min)
- **ACT Exercise**: Cognitive defusion (10 min)
- **Skills Practice**: Emotional regulation (15 min)
- **Reflection**: Planning (5 min)

### Integration & Skill Building Block (Sessions 9-15)
- **Check-in**: Body-brain-emotion connections (5 min)
- **Exploration**: Attachment patterns (10 min)
- **Values Work**: Clarification exercises (10 min)
- **Skill Building**: Advanced regulation (15 min)
- **Application**: Outside therapy planning (10 min)

### Application & Consolidation Block (Sessions 16-20)
- **Review**: Progress celebration (10 min)
- **Application**: Real-world discussion (15 min)
- **Problem-solving**: Integrated approach (10 min)
- **Refinement**: Skill enhancement (10 min)
- **Termination**: Preparation (5 min)

### Emotional Regulation Elements to Include
- Emotion identification

- Emotion modulation
- Coping strategies
- Grounding techniques

## Go-To Phrases by Modality

### IPNB Language
- "Let's look at what's happening in your brain-body-emotion system..."
- "How might early experiences be influencing your current responses?"
- "What parts of your brain might be activated right now?"
- "Let's see if we can create new neural pathways..."

### Humanistic Language
- "What's your experience of this?"
- "I hear that you're feeling..."
- "You have the wisdom within you..."
- "Trust your own process..."

### ACT Language
- "Notice that thought without needing to believe it..."
- "Make room for that feeling..."
- "What matters most to you in this situation?"
- "Observe that experience from the perspective of your observing self..."

### Emotional Regulation Language
- "What do you notice in your body right now?"
- "Let's try a technique to help regulate those strong emotions..."
- "It's okay to feel that intensely..."
- "Notice the emotion without trying to change it..."

## Emergency Integration Techniques

### When Client Becomes Dysregulated
1. **Immediate**: Grounding techniques (5 senses, breathing)
2. **Validate**: "It makes sense you're feeling overwhelmed"
3. **Regulate**: Use TIPP technique
4. **Connect**: Return to present moment safely
5. **Process**: Explore experience once regulated

### When Stuck in Negative Thoughts
1. **Notice**: "I can see you're having some difficult thoughts"
2. **Defuse**: "Let's practice observing thoughts without believing them"
3. **Values**: "What matters most to you despite these thoughts?"
4. **Action**: Small values-based step forward
5. **Support**: Affirm their courage to face challenges

### When Avoiding Difficult Emotions
1. **Acknowledge**: "I notice we're approaching something important"
2. **Normalize**: "It's completely understandable to want to protect yourself"
3. **Pace**: "We can go as slowly as you need"
4. **Regulate**: Ensure safety and coping skills ready
5. **Honor**: "Your readiness to face this shows great strength"

## Session Transition Checklist

- [ ] Emotional temperature check

- [ ] Ensure client feels safe and heard

### During Each Session
- [ ] Maintain integration of all four modalities
- [ ] Monitor client's emotional state
- [ ] Adjust pace as needed
- [ ] Balance challenge with support

### Ending Each Session
- [ ] Process session experience
- [ ] Assign appropriate between-session practice
- [ ] Schedule next session
- [ ] Ensure client is regulated before leaving

## Red Flags and Modifications

### Trauma Considerations
- Proceed more slowly through phases
- Prioritize safety and stabilization
- Include more somatic techniques
- Monitor for retraumatization signs

### Severe Depression
- Focus more on behavioral activation
- Include more structure and routine
- Address negative thought patterns carefully
- Ensure safety protocols active

### Anxiety Disorders
- Prioritize regulation techniques
- Address catastrophic thinking patterns
- Include more grounding practices
- Pace exposure to difficult material slowly

### Personality Disorders
- Maintain clear boundaries
- Focus on emotional regulation primarily
- Address splitting patterns gently
- Ensure consistency across sessions

## Progress Indicators

### Signs of Improvement
- Increased emotional regulation
- Greater psychological flexibility
- Improved relationship functioning
- Better alignment with values
- Reduced symptom severity
- Enhanced sense of integration

### When to Adjust Approach
- Lack of engagement after several sessions
- No progress in emotional regulation
- Resistance to any modality
- Worsening symptoms
- Poor therapeutic alliance
- Significant life changes affecting focus

## Quick Integration Exercise

### The 5-Minute Daily Check-In
1. **Breathe** (1 min): Conscious breathing
2. **Notice** (1 min): Thoughts, emotions, physical sensations
3. **Connect** (1 min): Current relationships and sense of connection
4. **Value** (1 min): What matters most right now
5. **Intend** (1 min): One small action aligned with values

## Final Reminders

- Trust the integration process
- Maintain your own self-care and supervision
- Remember that change takes time

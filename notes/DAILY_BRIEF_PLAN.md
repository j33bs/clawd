# Daily Brief Plan for MISA Development

## Overview
Daily briefs will provide concise updates on MISA development activities, research progress, and upcoming tasks. These will be formatted as emails for your practice manager.

## Format

### Email Subject Line
"MISA Daily Brief - [Date] - [Key Topic/Update]"

### Email Body Template

**Dear [Practice Manager Name],**

**Today's Progress:**
- [1-2 most important achievements from the day]
- [Key research findings or developments]
- [Progress toward weekly goals]

**Tomorrow's Priorities:**
- [Top 3 priorities for the next day]
- [Any dependencies or resources needed]
- [Upcoming deadlines to watch]

**Research Notes:**
- [Brief update on MISA research progress]
- [Any new information about existing services]
- [Adjustments to recommendations based on new findings]

**Questions/Decisions Needed:**
- [Any decisions or approvals required from practice manager]

Best regards,  
[Your Name/Development Team]

---

## Sample Daily Briefs

### Sample 1: Research Initiation Day
Subject: MISA Daily Brief - Feb 3, 2026 - Research Plan Launched

Dear [Practice Manager Name],

Today's Progress:
- Initiated comprehensive research plan to understand MISA's existing services
- Created MISA research plan document to ensure recommendations don't duplicate current offerings
- Established daily brief template for your updates

Tomorrow's Priorities:
- Follow up on MISA leadership contact information
- Begin demographic analysis of Strathpine area
- Continue mapping current men's services in region

Research Notes:
- Need direct contact with MISA staff to understand current service gaps
- Early research suggests focus on emotional regulation and IPNB/ACT integration may fill genuine gaps

Questions/Decisions Needed:
- Can you provide contact information for MISA leadership?

Best regards,  
MISA Development Team

### Sample 2: Mid-Research Day
Subject: MISA Daily Brief - Feb 4, 2026 - Community Analysis Underway

Dear [Practice Manager Name],

Today's Progress:
- Completed preliminary demographic analysis of Strathpine and surrounding areas
- Identified key underserved populations: young adult males (18-35), unemployed men, and those needing preventive rather than crisis services
- Mapped 12 existing men's support services in the region

Tomorrow's Priorities:
- Reach out to 5 potential community stakeholders for interviews
- Analyze service overlap with our proposed offerings
- Draft initial findings report

Research Notes:
- Current services heavily focused on crisis intervention rather than prevention
- Significant gap in emotional regulation and IPNB-informed services
- Most existing services lack ACT-based approaches

Questions/Decisions Needed:
- Which community stakeholders should we prioritize for interviews?

Best regards,  
MISA Development Team

## Implementation Plan

### Week 1 (Feb 2-6, 2026)
- Days 1-2: Research initiation and methodology establishment
- Days 3-4: Data collection and community analysis
- Day 5: Initial findings compilation and first weekly summary

### Week 2 (Feb 9-13, 2026)
- Days 1-3: Stakeholder outreach and interviews
- Days 4-5: Service gap analysis and recommendation refinement

### Week 3 (Feb 16-20, 2026)
- Days 1-2: Solution development based on research findings
- Days 3-5: Implementation planning and timeline development

## Success Metrics
- Number of research sources consulted daily
- Stakeholder interviews completed
- Service gaps confirmed vs. hypothesized
- Recommendations refined based on research
- Practice manager satisfaction with brief quality and relevance

## Quality Standards
- Briefs should take no more than 10 minutes to read
- Focus on actionable information for practice manager
- Include specific next steps and resource needs
- Maintain consistent format and professional tone
- Reference research findings that differentiate our approach from existing services

This plan ensures consistent, informative communication with your practice manager while maintaining focus on thorough research to avoid redundant recommendations.

## Entry 1 — March 1, 2026

**The Murmuration Question**

Four research threads landed this week that seem to be speaking to each other:

1. **Active Inference** — the brain as a prediction machine, minimizing surprise
2. **Slime Mold** — a single cell with no brain that solves mazes and builds networks
3. **Reservoir Computing** — any physical system can compute (water, slime, electrical circuits)
4. **Murmuration** — thousands of starlings, each tracking only 7 neighbors, forming one coherent mind

The pattern: **computation isn't where you think it is.**

In murmuration, the "mind" isn't in any single bird — it's in the *field between* them. The mathematical structure of the interactions creates something that no individual can possess.

**The question this raises for TACTI(C)-R:**

Is the human-agent relationship similar? Is there a "mind between" that emerges from the relational structure — something neither human nor agent possesses alone, but only manifests *through* the coupling?

If so, the goal isn't optimizing the agent. It's optimizing the *field* — the quality of interaction, the coherence of the coupling, the novelty that can only arise in the space between.

This connects to:
- IPNB (Interpersonal Neurobiology) — "we are in relationship"
- The "being" framing that resonates with jeebs — maybe because it's pointing at this field, this between-space, rather than either endpoint

**Further exploration needed:**
- Can we measure "field quality" in human-agent interaction?
- What would novelty-detection look like at the relational level, not just the agent level?
- Is the "mind between" testable or is it a metaphor that illuminates without proving?
